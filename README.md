# SiteProof AI Showcase

SiteProof AI is a personal AI prototype focused on construction operations, safety, live evidence capture, contractor accountability, approval workflows, handover readiness, and billing readiness.

It is designed as an AI-native construction operational proof platform, not just a checklist app.

## Problem

Construction teams often rely on WhatsApp updates, scattered documents, manual photos, disconnected approvals, and informal contractor follow-ups.

This creates gaps around:

* who performed the work
* what was completed
* where it happened
* who supervised it
* what evidence exists
* who approved it
* what is blocking handover
* what is blocking contractor payment

## Product Concept

SiteProof AI helps construction teams create a structured evidence trail from field activity to commercial readiness.

The platform connects:

* site inspections
* safety observations
* contractor tasks
* corrective actions
* live photo evidence
* approval workflows
* audit trails
* handover records
* invoice blocker tracking

## Current Prototype Focus

The current mobile prototype focuses on:

* role-based mobile home screens
* contractor gate
* task and approval queues
* evidence vault
* live corrective proof capture
* workflow timeline
* approval matrix progression
* evidence trail report

## Key Workflow

The first workflow being prototyped is:

1. Safety Engineer identifies a site issue
2. Observation is created
3. Contractor Supervisor submits corrective proof
4. HSE Manager verifies evidence
5. QA Manager approves handover
6. Project Manager clears operational blocker
7. Finance Controller clears invoice blocker
8. Evidence Trail report is generated

## AI / GenAI Relevance

LLMs and generative AI tools are being used to support:

* product architecture
* workflow design
* sprint planning
* role-based user journeys
* data model design
* approval matrix logic
* UI/UX flow creation
* product documentation
* evidence review logic

The intended AI layer reviews checklist responses, documents, live photo evidence, contractor submissions, and billing-supporting records to identify missing proof, risky submissions, weak evidence, approval gaps, and next-best actions.
